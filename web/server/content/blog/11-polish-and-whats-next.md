If the earlier posts were about big new rooms, this one's about the finishing work — the trim, the door handles, the lights that turn on when you walk in.

A recent run of improvements, mostly aimed at the everyday feel of the app:

- **Mobile, properly.** A bottom navigation bar, a tidier header, and fixes so nothing overflows or gets clipped on a small screen. Stash Squirrel should feel at home on your phone, not just your desktop.
- **Jumping between views.** Hop from a todo to its place on the calendar and straight back again — the list and calendar now talk to each other.
- **A "due today" popup** to greet you at sign-in with what's on your plate (switchable off, if that's not your thing).
- **A dedicated settings page** to keep those toggles in one sensible place.
- **Reset your password** from the login page, for the inevitable day you forget it.
- Plus a long tail of smaller things — a **live demo** you can explore without signing up, a **landing page** with a little squirrel film, tidier dialogs, and countless quiet bug fixes.

### And this blog

Which brings us to now — and to *you*. This blog exists so I can tell you what I'm working on and, more importantly, hear back. I'm building Stash Squirrel largely on my own, guided by what people actually find useful.

So: what should I build next? What's clumsy? What's missing? What do you love? There's a feedback link on every post, and I read every message. Thanks for being here — it means a lot.
