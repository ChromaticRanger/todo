A while back I wrote about building a [browser extension](/blog/browser-extension) — a Stash Squirrel button that lives in your toolbar so you can save the page you're looking at without leaving it. Until now, installing it meant a bit of a faff: download a file, flip on developer mode, load it by hand. Fine for the curious, not for everyone.

That changes today. **The Stash Squirrel extension is now published on the Chrome Web Store.**

[Add it to Chrome →](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/stash-squirrel/inhkmokpomefeajkjpedkljnjfpddkhp)

One click to install, and you're done. Sign in once to link it to your account, and from then on any page worth keeping is a single click away — title, link and all — straight into a list of your choosing.

![Saving the current page from the browser toolbar with the Stash Squirrel extension](/blog-images/browser-extension.png)

Getting through review was its own small adventure — icons at exactly the right size, screenshots, a privacy write-up, and instructions so the reviewers could see it work. It's a good feeling to have it out there properly.

If you install it, I'd genuinely love to know how it fits into your day — and if there's another browser you wish it supported, tell me. There's a feedback link on every post, and I read every message.
