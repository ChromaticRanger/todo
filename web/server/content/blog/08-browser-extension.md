Bookmarks have a timing problem. You find a great page, mean to save it "later", and later never comes. By the time you're back in your organiser, the moment — and often the tab — is gone.

The fix is to close the gap between *finding* something and *saving* it. So I built a **browser extension**.

Now, when you're on a page worth keeping, you click the Stash Squirrel icon and it's saved to your account then and there — title, link and all — without leaving the page. No copying URLs, no switching tabs.

![Saving the current page from the browser toolbar with the Stash Squirrel extension](/blog-images/browser-extension.png)

A couple of things I care about with the extension:

- **It only looks when you ask it to.** The extension reads the page you're on *only* when you click to save. It doesn't watch your browsing, and there's no analytics or tracking in it. (The privacy policy spells this out in full.)
- **It stays out of the way.** One click, a little confirmation, done.

It's a small piece of the puzzle, but it's the one that makes the "stash" in Stash Squirrel effortless. The whole app is more useful when saving something costs you nothing.

If you use the extension, I'd love to know how it fits into your day — and if there's a browser you wish it supported, tell me.
