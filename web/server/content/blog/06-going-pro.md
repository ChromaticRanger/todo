At some point a side project has to answer an awkward question: how does it keep the lights on?

For Stash Squirrel, the answer is a simple two-tier model. There's a **free** plan that's genuinely useful on its own, and a **Pro** plan for the people who want the extra powers and are happy to support the project while they're at it. Payments run through Stripe, and around the same time I added proper **email verification** so accounts start on a solid footing.

I thought hard about where the line between free and Pro should sit, and I keep coming back to one rule: **the free plan should never feel broken.** It's not a crippled trial designed to nag you into upgrading. It's a real tool you can happily use forever. Pro is for going further, not for unlocking basic dignity.

Being a solo developer, a modest paid tier is also what makes it sensible to keep pouring time into this — every improvement in these posts exists partly because a few kind people decided it was worth a few pounds a month.

If you're on the fence about Pro, or you think something behind it really ought to be free (or vice versa), I'd genuinely like to hear your reasoning. These lines aren't set in stone.
