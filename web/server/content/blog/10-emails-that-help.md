An organiser only works if you come back to it. That's not a given — life is busy and tabs get closed. So I added a couple of quiet, well-mannered emails to keep the loop closed.

**A welcome email** now greets you once you've verified your account and signed in for the first time. It's a friendly hello, a quick tour of what's here, and a nudge toward the bits people often miss. New folks shouldn't have to go hunting to find the good stuff.

**A daily digest** is the one I'm fondest of. Each morning it can send you a short summary of what's on your plate that day — so the things you carefully added actually resurface when they matter, instead of quietly ageing in a list. A few details I sweated over:

- It arrives at **7am in your own timezone**, not mine — a morning email at 2am helps nobody.
- There's a **catch-up window**, so a hiccup on my end doesn't mean a silently missed day.
- It comes from a real **support address** you can just reply to, not a no-reply void.

And of course, if digests aren't for you, they're entirely optional — you're in control of what lands in your inbox.

If there's a moment where a small nudge from Stash Squirrel would genuinely help — or one where I'm at risk of being annoying — that's exactly the sort of thing I want to get right. Tell me.
